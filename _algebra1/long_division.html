--- title: Long Division order: 1 layout: mathjax --- Long Division

When \(a \ne 0\), there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are $$x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.$$ There may be one (repeated) real root, two (distinct) real roots, or complex roots (complex conjugates), which are /(a \plusminus b i /).
